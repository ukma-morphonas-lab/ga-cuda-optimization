\section*{Hypothesis: CUDA-Based Parallelization in Genetic Algorithms}

The application of CUDA technology to parallelize computations in genetic algorithms 
will enable significant acceleration of key operations: fitness function evaluation, 
selection, crossover, and mutation - compared to sequential implementation on the CPU. 
It is expected that parallelization efficiency will increase proportionally to population 
size and computational complexity of the fitness function, providing a substantial reduction in execution time for these operations, with speedup magnitude depending on population size and fitness function complexity.

\bigskip Parallel processing of large populations on the GPU is expected to improve the quality of 
solutions found, due to enabling operation with significantly larger population sizes without 
a critical increase in overall algorithm execution time. This is crucial for opening up new 
possibilities to solve complex optimization problems in acceptable timeframes.
